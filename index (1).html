Who We Are

Badalte Bharat ki Nayi Tasveer

NayePankh Foundation began in 2020, when a group of high schoolers felt the urge to help during the COVID-19 pandemic. On 28th March 2021, we officially launched to serve as the youth of a rapidly progressing nation — because the hope of a nation lies in the hands of its youth.

Today, "NayePankh" — meaning new wings — is a UP Government, 12A & 80G registered NGO, completely led by youth, many still in school or college. We provide food, sanitary napkins, clothes, and education to underprivileged communities, and run awareness campaigns on menstrual hygiene.

What We Do

How we uplift communities

From food drives to education and menstrual hygiene awareness, every initiative is run by youth volunteers for the people who need it most.

Free Food Distribution

Fighting hunger by distributing free food to underprivileged communities — and to stray animals as well.

Sanitary Pad Drives

Providing sanitary napkins and creating awareness campaigns on menstrual hygiene for women and youth.

Clothes for the Poor

Collecting and distributing clothes to poor families across the communities we serve.

Education Drives

Educating underprivileged sections of society to build a better future for the next generation.

Youth-Led Volunteering

Run entirely by students and youth volunteers from schools and colleges across UP and beyond.

Tax-Exempt Giving

As a 12A & 80G certified NGO, donations to NayePankh qualify for tax relief under the Indian Income Tax Act.

Our Journey

From a pandemic response to a movement

What started as a small group of high schoolers trying to help during COVID-19 has grown into one of India's biggest student-led NGOs.

2020

A group of high schoolers felt the urge to help during the COVID-19 pandemic and started providing relief with whatever resources they had.

28 Mar 2021

NayePankh Foundation officially launched on the ground, taking the name "new wings" — to uplift the underprivileged.

Expansion

Operations grew to Kanpur, Ghaziabad, and other cities, adding sanitary pad drives, clothes distribution, and education programs.

Today

A UP Government, 12A & 80G registered NGO that has helped over 2 lakh underprivileged people — and counting.
